Center of "Tierra de Campos"

English translation is in progress and may be not accurate

It is located between the rivers Cea and Valderaduey, which it shares with other neighboring municipalities as Cea, Grajal Campos or Calzada del Coto. It's the main town of the judicial district. Located on the plains of the river Cea and Valderaduey, born in the shadow of the Facundo and Primitivo martyrs sanctuary and consolidated in the eleventh century with the arrival of the Order of Cluny and the grant of jurisdiction to the town by king Alfonso VI. During his reign Sahagún gained importance with the impetus given to the pilgrimage route and the introduction into the peninsula cluniacenses rites and uses against the Visigoth, which led to increased trade and extended the influence of the town over a large geographic area. After maintaining its importance during the Middle Ages, the decline of the town was progressive, accentuating it by the confiscation of Mendizabal. In 1931 it was the second Spanish town after Eibar (Vasque Country) proclaiming the Second Spanish Republic. It's heritage and monuments, and various celebrations taking place throughout the year, most notably Easter, make it a site receiving domestic and international tourism. Among its most representative monuments are the Church of San Lorenzo, the church of San Tirso, the Sanctuary of the Pilgrim Virgin and the ruins of the Royal Monastery of San Benito, all declared of Cultural Interest.



Click map to enlarge this image



This cultural importance is accentuated by being a place of the Road to Santiago and belonging to the Federation of Cluniac Sites. In 2009 the town celebrated the ninth centenary of the death of King Alfonso VI, various commemorative activities were carried out like conferences and publications.

Road to Santiago

Click on title to expand/collapse this section

Sahagún is at the geographic center of the Road to Santaigo -french route- and that's why we receive thousands of people every year. This one of the main economic activities in this town.



The route goes from East to West, giving to the pilgrims the chance to visit most of our monuments and museums (give a look at the monuments section) and lodging for pilgrims at public hostel at "Iglesia de la Trinidad" -Trinity church- or Domus Viatoris -private hostel option-
There are many other lodging options at several hotels all over the town.



Sahagún is the starting point of the "Vía Trajana", an alternative subroute of the French route of Road to Santiago which goes throught other little towns close to Sahagún, giving chance to the pilgrim a new flavour in his/her way to Santiago.

We join two Roads to Santiago: the Madrid route and the french route (you can check them by clicking at the route map).




Monuments

Click on title to expand/collapse this section

San Lorenzo church


View on maps

It's the main church in this town, it has both romanic and mudejar building styles, it was build in the 12nd century.

It has basilica shape and three main stages, the main one has golden arcs. The tower -next to the absid- was built in the 14th century, and it has four main areas. Next to the church you'll find the Jesus chapel -Easter Museum-, where the biblical statues from Easter celebrations -processions- are showed all the year.

In 2011, the church was seriously damaged, the "Hispania Nostra" group included it in a "red code list" of damaged buildings. In 2014, was completely fixed and now one of the must visits at the town.


San Tirso church


View on maps

Built with romanic-mudejar mixed styles, it was started in the 12nd century, the apse was started usign rock and then church was completed using briks, there are another two apses on the side wings but they are already build using briks.
The main tower has squared shape over the cross area and it has four levels over it. Inside the church there are three areas which have a inside-wooden roof.

This is the most important mudejar style building in Sahagún.


Sanctuary of Virgen Peregrina


View on maps

This sanctuary was build in 1257 as an Franciscan convent after being agreed by the Spanish King Alfonso X in 1245. It was completely build using bricks following the "romanic-mudejar" style which today still can see the church and some part of the cloister, both of them were fixed many times.

The church was built with only a main stage but divided in five areas: three capiles, the cross and the absid. We can see many old details in the outdoor furniture coming from the south of Spain.

The convent was abanoned in 1835 due to the Mendizábal process in Spain, where many goods from the Catolic church were given back to the town.
This process took place all over the country in this year. After that, the temple was slowly damaged for years.





In 2006, the City Council of Sahagún and the Spanish Government signed and agreement to fix the sancturay, they will try to turn it in the "Documentation Center about the Road to Santiago" which was finished in 2010.





During the fixing work, a mummy was found most likely from the 15th century, there are no close clues about who is this person, some experts guess that he could be some major monk.


Royal Monastery of San Benito


View on maps

This monastery was stablished by the Cluny order, it was the main point of the religious transformation which took place at León and Castile kingdoms during the 11th century. It's story started with the king Alfonso III when he bought an already existent church which contained the relics of saints Facundo and Primitivo to give them to the abad Alonso who was running out from Córdoba.





In 1080, the King Alfonso VI donated it to the Cluny order which started the best years of this monastery, later, when the King Alfonso VI died, he was buried here as well as his wives but one.

In the XV century, the Monastery of San Benito de Valladolid was in charge and it started going down quickly, in 1820 was completely closed during the Trienio Liberal years.



Remaning parts of the old monastery

San Mancio Capile

Formed by one stage divided in three areas -but the last one was lost during years- it was on the north of the absid which was built on the 12th century.



San Benito arch

Built in 1662 and designed by the architect Felipe Berrojo, it was built to replace the previous one romanic door which was already ruined.



Tower watch


There were two "twin" towers here next to the monastery but only one of them remains standing, later, the City Council installed a big clock on the last one.

There is some confusing data about the second tower which points to another direction, maybe the second one was started but never completed, but data is not conclusive.

Trinidad church / Public pilgrim hostel / Carmelo Gómez auditorium / Tourist info office


View on maps

At this point you'll find four things in a row.

This temple was built between the 16th and 17th centuries, but the tower was already built for a previous ruined building in the 13th century. It has one big only stage, without cross and an internal elevated chapel.
It was quite damaged in the year 1964, so, the people from Sahagún stopped to use it.

It was closed for years until 1993 when the repair works started to be used again as: Pilgrim hostel, Turist info office and Auditorium "Carmelo Gómez", today is used to as public pilgrim hostel and tourist info office.


Santa Cruz church and monastery


View on maps

Located inside the Benedictine sisters convent, you'll find the well-known altarpieze created in 1711 by Joaquín Benito Churriguera.

Moved from the Royal Monastery of San Benito, you can find too the sepultures of King Alfonso VI and four of his wives -Inés de Aquitania, Constanza de Borgoña, Berta de Borgoña-Maçon and Zaida-.

The monastery also contains an sculpture of "Virgen Peregrina" and a master-pieze called "Custodia" from 1556 found at the Royal Monastery of San Benito too, created by Enrique de Arfe (You'll find further information about it at the museum section).





Here you can buy the pastries called "Amargillos" -almond pastries- and more home-made food -most of them pastries- by the Benedictine Sisters, just ask them about it.



Virgen del Puente hermitage


View on maps

It's located 2 km far away to the town next to a medieval bridge near to the Valderaduey river, it's the first building you'll see if you came from the Road to Santiago -french route- in this province.

It was built in the 12th century using mudejar style, it was intended to be pilgrim hostel and hospital. It's only an hermitage nowadays.

It was quite damaged in the last years, so, it was repaired between 2009 and 2010.

Those statues show both king Alfonso VI and Bernardo de Sedirac, 2nd abad Sahagún monastery, cofounder of this town and archbishop of Toledo, he promoted the french route of the Road to Santiago.


San Juan of Sahagún church


View on maps

Neoclassical-colonial styled, was completed in 1652 using stone and clay, during the 18th century was painted using white color.

The church was build in the house once owned by San Juan parents, inside this building we can see many piezes about San Juan created by Gregorio Fernández.


Puente Canto


View on maps

It was built just over the Cea river, its origins dates from Rome but was completely destroyed and built again in 1085 under Alfonso VI kingdom and fixed/repaired many times over the years, in the 16th century by Francisco Aguilar and the 19th century by Eduardo Galán Mendizábal.

It's build using rock and stone, it has five archs over the river.

Gastronomy

Click on title to expand/collapse this section

Cuisine from this town and its area is delicious and abundant, today one major reasons to visit our restaurants
There are many Conferences and Fairs during the year about our cuisine.



The leek is the flagship product of this town. Annually, every October is celebrated the "San Simon" fair.
The monks from Cluny Order were the first to bring leeks to the Sahagún area, monks of the Benedictine convent began its cultivation in the whole area of Cea river. Sahagún leeks have whiteness, tenderness and a special flavor and orchards where they grow watered by the River Cea, which distinguishes them from others.


There are many other Sahagún specialties as legumes:lentils, beans and chickpeas. Diverse meat from hunting like hares, partridges, quail, pigeons..., snails and crabs are quite famous too.
Yeah! Really! You can taste snails stew and crab stew in some restaurants, but you may need to request it in advance.


Red and rose wines from León -called "Tierras de León" wines which are made from grapes Prieto Picudo and Mencía are produced in the nearby, which are becaming important recently in this area.



Sweets and pastries:
You must try some sweets such as "amarguillos" (almond pastries above this lines), "iron biscuits" (photo under this lines) -don't be scared! :) iron name is just from the tool from which they are made-.




Celebrations / Holidays

Click on title to expand/collapse this section

Easter



Sahagun Easter was declared of Regional Tourist Interest. Through it, the Cofrafía Jesus Nazareno and San Jose Sponsorship through the streets of the town on Palm Sunday, Holy Thursday, Good Friday and Easter Sunday.


Processions


It begins with the auction of the procession statues -people can buy the rights about statues procession by public auction-, event held at 5 pm at the Jesus Chapel next to the Church of San Lorenzo, the Sunday before "Palm Sunday" also known as "domingo tortillero". The most expensive statues should be the Virgen de la Soledad and the Cristo de la Urna.



A week later, on Palm Sunday, blessing takes place in the Chapel of San Juan and then the procession of palms takes place at the Church of San Lorenzo, with the statue of Jesus entering Jerusalem bid for the youth of the brotherhood.


On Holy Wednesday afternoon a solemn and massive "Besapies" -kissing Jesus feet ceremony- to the image of Jesus of Nazareth takes place. At the end, from the Benedictine Sisters convent starts a processional ordeal that crosses the old town with the image of "Cristo de los Entierros"- XVI century-.



Holy Thursday evening, the Brotherhood of Vera Cruz in procession from the Church of San Lorenzo to St. Tirso church processions the "Oración del Huerto" and the Ecce Homo -Jesus tied to the Column-, Jesus with the Cross, "Virgen de las Amarguras" and "Virgen de la Soledad". In the halfway, in the Sahagun main square, the band played the anthem "Virgen de las Amarguras". After the procession in the Plaza de San Tirso, the act of farewell in which the statues "Oración de Huerto" -Jesus" and "Virgen de la Soledad" move their arms, after the end of the procession the "Ronda" starts. The same day, at 12 pm, the sound of the horn is made from the tower of S. Lorenzo.



El Good Friday, at 9 in the morning it takes place the act known as La Isa, consisting of opening the doors of the chapel on wings catching whoever is nearby and pushing the door with their feet. Then begins the procession of steps (known by the Jews) which is the most popular of all; it is borne in procession steps larger and heavier. these, in order are: -The Cirineo- Jesus Nazareno (popularly known as The Trunk); then Jesus on Golgotha ​​(known for The majito hole); then Las Tres Marias, after Jesus Crucified (better known by the horse Longinus) and finally Descent. Formerly, each of these steps was carried by a different union; well, The Trunk was carried by the gardeners, El Barreno Majito carrying the carpenters, the Three Marias carry the fifth, Longinus Horse-the heaviest of all-what had Descent fishermen and shepherds.


The same day in the afternoon, at the end of the Mass, and after performing the act of "Desenclavo" -removing Jesus from cross- and moving the body of the Nazarene in the urn, the procession of the Holy Burial starts, this is the most solemn and sad procession. Halfway, in the main square, the choir and the band plays the "Cristo de la Urna" and the "Virgen de la Soledad" songs, those are quite appreciated by people of this town.



Easter Acts conclude on Easter Sunday, with the Procession of "el encuentro" -Jesus and Maria meeting after resurrection- . The Virgin starts procession from the Church of San Tirso, while Jesus does from the San Lorenzo church. Both meet at the Plaza Mayor -main square-. Following this there will be other changes the Virgin swap her mantle from dark to lighter colors. Finally both head towards the Church of S. Tirso together.



Three weeks after the pilgrimage takes place in "Pastor Bono" -Good shepherd- and "Patrocinio de San Jose", dedicated to the shepherds; Pastors of Joarilla de las Matas, staged the traditional dance of the handles, a folk legacy dance. Subsequently the Brotherhood of Jesus of Nazareth offers a snack to all attendees. In the afternoon, in the "Plantio" -close to sports area- sweets and lemonade - CAREFUL! lemonade in Sahagun means wine with lemon, not for children!- are offered for free with traditional flute music.



April



Every April 25th the festival of San Marcos takes place, also called bread and cheese celebration is held next to the "Virgen del Puente" hermitage. In it, besides the procession of the Virgin, bread and cheese are tasted for free to all atendees, accompanied by lemonade - CAREFUL! lemonade in Sahagun means wine with lemon, not for children!- and enlivened by dulzaineros -traditional music-. In the afternoon, the rosary is celebrated and a tasting of snails is offered -you should try this, it has great taste, it's a traditional food-.


June




On June 12th, during four or five days (regarding on the closer weekend to this day), the main celebration in Sahagún takes place, "San Juan de Sahagún" -you'll find San Juan de Sahagún at the "outstanding people of sahagun" section-, where you can enjoy bullfighting in the arena and run-street-bullfighting and many other celebrations.

During this days all the people from Sahagún and many other towns close to Sahagún will celebrate together during all day and night partying and some days you'll be able to enjoy fireworks too.







July



In July, several events take place; on June 2nd we celebrate the "Virgen Peregrina" day, after the procession to her sanctuary, "bollos preñaos" -bread with red sausagge- and lemonade - CAREFUL! lemonade in Sahagun means wine with lemon, not for children!- are offered. Furthermore, in the first weekend of the month a show related to the motor world takes place; in the second weekend of the District Day is celebrated with popular food and nightlife, and the last weekend of the month takes place the day of the "Peñas" -peñas is a group of people which participates at the San Juan celebration- and associations with popular food and party.



In the third weekend of this month, Jugglers meeting is celebrated, which has been held since 2002 and aims to remember the first school of Jugglers of Spain, founded in the town in 1116.




August

The festival of San Lorenzo takes place on August 10th. The 15th, the Feast of the vacationer, dedicated to promoting the attractions of the town among visitors. Paella cooking competition and tasting "bollo preñao" -bread with red sausagge-, the night is enlivened with a verbena -street night party-.


September



In September the Jacobean Route Bikers Concentration takes place, but there is not exact date for it, it changes from year to year.


October



October 28th, Feria de San Simon is celebrated, also called Fair Leek, where food products are exposed, agricultural machinery and equipment next to the Sahagun sports area.


Saturday street market (weekly)



Dispite of all this events, every Saturaday in a weekly basis, you can enjoy a street market at the main streets of the village. Were you will be able to buy some fresh vegetables or fruits, clothes or whatever you need.

San Juan de Sahagún

(1430-1479) Augustinian friar, was canonized by Pope Alexander VIII, and is the patron saint of the towns of Sahagun and Salamanca.

Bernardino de Sahagún

(1499-1590) Franciscan friar, author of bilingual works in Nahuatl and Spanish, considered valuable for reconstructing the history of ancient Mexico.

Pedro Ponce de León

(antes de 1508-1584) Teacher and Benedictine monk.

Fernando de Castro

(1814-1874) Philosopher, educator and intellectual, academic of the Royal Academy of History and director of the University of Madrid between 1868 and 1870.

Benito Pamparacuatro

(1897-1936) Major during the Second Republic in 2006 was granted the title of favorite son of the town.

Joaquín González Cuenca

(*1942) Professor of Spanish Literature at the University of Castilla-La Mancha.

Javier Velasco Rodríguez

José Luna Borge
